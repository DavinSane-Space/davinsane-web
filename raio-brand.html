01 The Brief

RAIO was launching into the B2B operations analytics market with no visual identity. The product was technically strong — built on real-time data infrastructure — but the brand had to communicate something that SaaS brands often fail to: that precision and intelligence can coexist with warmth and accessibility.

The brief: build a system that works across the product UI, investor pitch decks, conference booth graphics, and digital marketing — without ever feeling like it was built for any one of these formats specifically. The name "RAIO" (Portuguese for "ray" or "beam") provided the visual entry point.

02 Concept

A competitive audit of the operations analytics space found a market dominated by generic SaaS blue — safe, corporate, interchangeable. The opportunity was to stand apart with a brand that felt precise and scientific without being cold or inaccessible.

The concept emerged from the word itself: RAIO as a beam of focused light — signal cutting through noise. The visual metaphor became the structural basis for the mark: a geometric ray that carries both directionality (progress, forward motion) and precision (a single, sharp form rather than a diffuse shape). The mark is a condensed letter R constructed so that it reads as both a letterform and an angular beam.

Brand concept — mark construction based on the geometric ray metaphor

03 System

The color system is built on a deep midnight base with a single high-energy signal accent — an electric amber that reads as data, energy, and alert simultaneously. This combination is rare in B2B software and immediately differentiating in competitive contexts.

The motion system was built in two registers. The first is the brand ident: a 4-second logo animation where the mark assembles from geometric primitives, suggesting the assembly of data into signal. The second is a generative data visualization style used in marketing materials — abstract representations of operational metrics rendered as dynamic ray patterns, each one unique and driven by real RAIO data exports.

04 Application

The brand system ships as a Figma library covering all marketing and communications touchpoints: slide templates, social post formats, email headers, event booth graphics, and product marketing assets. A brand guidelines document covers usage rules, typography, color, motion timing, and photography direction.

Product application was handled through a token handoff to the product design team — the brand system's color primitives map directly to the UI token system, ensuring that what users see in the product and what they see in marketing materials feel like they come from the same source.
