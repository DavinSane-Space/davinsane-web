01 Overview

Toyota authorized dealers in Latin America operate across five disconnected software systems to manage their daily business a legacy DMS for inventory, a separate CRM for customer records, a third tool for service scheduling, a fourth for financial reporting, and a fifth for parts ordering. The cognitive overhead of switching between these systems cost dealers an estimated 40 minutes per salesperson per day.

The Toyota Dealer Hub is the unified platform designed to replace that fragmented stack. The goal was not to build a feature-complete ERP it was to build the operational layer that dealers actually touch during customer-facing interactions, with deep links to backend systems for the rest.

02 The Problem

Contextual observation at four dealerships in two countries revealed the real workflow: a salesperson helping a customer configure a new vehicle had to open three different applications to answer basic questions about available stock, financing options, and delivery timelines. In multiple observed sessions, this context-switching caused the salesperson to lose the customer's attention entirely.

Service advisors faced a parallel problem: they couldn't see a customer's full vehicle history, open recalls, and scheduled appointment in the same view. Information that should have informed every service interaction was scattered across two systems with no shared identifier.

Contextual observation shadowing sales and service teams across four dealerships

03 Process

The design process began with a role mapping exercise identifying the six distinct roles that interact with dealer operations software (general manager, sales manager, salesperson, service advisor, service technician, parts coordinator) and mapping the distinct set of information each role needs at each touchpoint in the customer journey.

From this mapping, a clear architectural principle emerged: the system needed to be role-aware, not just role-permissioned. A salesperson and a service advisor logging into the same dealership should see completely different default views not the same interface with some items greyed out.

04 Solution

The Dealer Hub presents each user with a role-configured home screen on login. A salesperson sees their pipeline, today's scheduled test drives, and the top 10 most-searched models in their dealership's inventory. A service advisor sees the day's appointments, active service bays, and any vehicles awaiting parts.

The vehicle detail view the most used screen in the system shows inventory, financing scenarios, available accessories, and delivery timeline in a single scroll. Salespeople can configure a vehicle and generate a quote without leaving the screen, and the quote is automatically saved to the customer's record.
