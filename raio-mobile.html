App Field Ops

RAIO Mobile

A field operations companion app for RAIO built offline-first so that teams working in remote or low-connectivity environments never lose access to critical operational data or the ability to log field events.

Product Designer

Year

RAIO Mobile offline-first field operations app with sync status indicators

01 Overview

RAIO's field operations teams technicians, inspectors, and supervisors working in warehouses, construction sites, and remote infrastructure needed access to the same operational data available on the desktop dashboard, but in environments where connectivity is unreliable or absent for hours at a time.

The RAIO Mobile app was designed around an offline-first architecture: every piece of data the user needs for their shift is downloaded at the start of the day, and every action taken in the field is queued locally until connectivity is restored. The design challenge was making this invisible to the user the experience should feel continuous, not interrupted.

02 The Problem

The existing solution was a mobile-responsive version of the desktop web app which meant field workers were pinching to zoom on data tables, struggling with hover-dependent interactions on a touchscreen, and completely unable to use the app when they lost cellular signal. Teams had resorted to paper logs, which then had to be manually transcribed into RAIO at the end of each shift.

The paper-to-digital transcription step was estimated to consume 90 minutes per supervisor per day across RAIO's customer base. Beyond the time cost, data entered 6 8 hours after the fact introduced errors and made real-time alerting impossible for field events.

Field research observing operations teams at three industrial sites with varying connectivity

03 Process

Field research at three industrial sites a logistics warehouse, a construction project, and an energy installation shaped the core interaction principles. Workers wore gloves in two of the three environments. Screens were viewed in direct sunlight. Logging events had to be completable in under 30 seconds, because interrupting a physical task for longer than that created safety issues.

These constraints produced a design language radically different from the desktop product: large touch targets (minimum 56dp), high-contrast colors optimized for sunlight readability, and a simplified event logging flow that uses voice input, photo attachment, and checkbox selection as primary inputs minimizing the need for text entry in difficult conditions.

04 Solution

RAIO Mobile downloads the user's task list, site data, and relevant alerts at the start of each shift. A persistent connectivity indicator in the header communicates sync status at all times green when live, amber when operating from cached data. Actions taken offline are shown with a subtle queued indicator, and sync happens automatically the moment connectivity is restored.

Conflict resolution the case where two users update the same record while offline was designed with a human in the loop: when a sync conflict is detected, the app presents both versions and asks the user to choose, rather than resolving silently in the background and potentially discarding real field data.
